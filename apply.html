Open Application

Founded in 2009, Suck Ventures is dedicated to building the future through technology, supporting innovators at all stages. We are looking for passionate individuals to join our mission of supporting bold ideas and entrepreneurs in various fields. We value diverse skills, experiences, and perspectives to help build transformative technology for the future.

If you believe you have what it takes to be part of our team and shape the future, apply here. We're always looking for talent in technology, operations, marketing, community management, and more.

The Role

In this open application, we're not focusing on a specific role—rather, we want to hear from you about how you can contribute. Whether you are a developer, designer, marketer, or have expertise in a different domain, we value individuals who are excited about technology and are eager to innovate. This is your opportunity to tell us what unique strengths you bring and how you envision yourself making an impact.

Voluntary Self-Identification of Disability

We are a federal contractor or subcontractor. The law requires us to provide equal employment opportunity to qualified people with disabilities. We have a goal of having at least 7% of our workers as people with disabilities. The law says we must measure our progress towards this goal. To do this, we must ask applicants and employees if they have a disability or have ever had one. People can become disabled, so we need to ask this question at least every five years.

Pay Transparency Nondiscrimination Provision

Suck Ventures will not discharge or otherwise discriminate against employees or applicants because they have inquired about, discussed, or disclosed their own pay or the pay of another employee or applicant. However, employees who have access to the compensation information of other employees or applicants as a part of their essential job functions cannot disclose the pay of other employees or applicants to individuals who do not otherwise have access to compensation information, unless such disclosure is (a) in response to a formal complaint or charge, (b) in furtherance of an investigation, proceeding, hearing, or action, including an investigation conducted by the employer, or (c) consistent with the company’s legal duty to furnish information.
